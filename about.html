About

— Commitment to create user-centered designs that evoke emotion and action.

My name is Hee Yeon Hwang and I am a User Experience Designer with a strong background in psychology research. In 2017, I graduated with a B.S. in Psychology from University of California, San Diego as a researcher in two labs focusing on emotion. I then soon became a lab manager at the Emotion and Mental Health Lab at Washington University in St. Louis and managed a study on how people in their everyday lives manage their own emotions and how their emotions impact their decisions. However, I craved creativity and to see quicker results of my labor in my career.

With my new path in UX design and my background in psychology research, I learned to focus on design that evokes feelings and actions. I want to create design that makes users feel satisfied and want to use again when using my products. I also learned to prioritize the most important features. I focus on the key functions of users’ needs first before the aesthetic part of the design in my iterative process. I transfer my research experience and project management skills to provide user-centered designs to users to solve their issues from their perspective.

I am currently seeking UX opportunities in the west coast and willing to relocate.
