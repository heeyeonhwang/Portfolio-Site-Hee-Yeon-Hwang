Ink Tank

An app for tattoo lovers.

1. The Challenge

Ink Tank was a project created for the CareerFoundry UX Design Certification. It is an app for tattoo lovers to find tattoo design inspirations and nearby artists. Ink Tank was created to help users streamline the process from filtering through categories such as specialties, prices, and reviews, to comparing artists and booking sessions. This project is a native app to help users who are busy and on the go.

  • Process: User Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, User Testing
  • Tools: Design Thinking, Material Design, Figma, Adobe XD
  • Duration: August 2019 - February 2020 (6 months)

2. Competitive & User Research

For Ink Tank, I conducted initial competitive analysis and analyzed 71 surveys and four interviews through affinity maps.

I analyzed the two apps, Tattoodo and InkSquad. I focused my competition analysis on Tattoodo since it is the biggest community in the market. Through this competitive UX analysis, Ink Tank has an opportunity to make the app free, improve on the messaging, explore, and search features.

For my user research, one challenge I faced during was my unfamiliarity in the tattoo culture. So I reached out to my personal connections and communities, such as Reddit's r/tattoos, filled out the initial survey. This initial analysis uncovered what users’ needs and wants and led me to prioritize the big features such as a filtering system for tattoo artists based on their location, price, and reviews.

3. User Personas

Two user personas were created with the research analyses. Creating Janice and Bryan further made me focus on Ink Tank’s core features, filtering system based on artists’ specialties, reviews, price, and location, and an easy booking system with the artist.

4. User Flows & Sitemap

User flows and the sitemap were created for Janice and Bryan’s objectives. The sitemap of Ink Tank was revised after the card sorting analysis. Five participants participated an open card sort via Optimal and each session took about three and a half minutes each. Card sorting for the sitemap helped include more connections to each feature that I had not originally thought of before.

5. Wireframing

The initial wireframe was created via Figma for both mobile and desktop versions of Ink Tank. I chose to self-learn Figma and Adobe XD to help accelerate my ability to prototype quickly to test with users.

6. Usability Testing

The usability test results showed us that Ink Tank still needed improvements on its user experience design. The main problem it faced was the confusion of the hamburger icon to filter through search categories. All six participants encountered challenges figuring out this feature. Changing the hamburger icon into a universally recognized filter icon helped users understand the filter features of the app.

7. Final Mock-up

Throughout the iterative process for Ink Tank, the overall transformation focused on making it as minimal as possible. Since the app showcases a variety of colorful and busy pictures of tattoos, the rest of the app needed a step back. The minimalism was pushed mostly by following the Material Design guidelines. Material Design taught me about prioritizing certain aspects of Ink Tank to minimize the design of the rest of the features.

8. Design Language & Documentation

In order to maintain a consitent design for future iterations of Ink Tank, I also created a design language documenation. This language sets the standard of the overarching rules for the overall product. These rules apply to the color pallette, UI elements, language, tone of voice, accessibility, grids and layouts, typography, and iconography.

9. Prototype & Next Steps

The next big step for Ink Tank would be to utilize Kep Performance Indicators (KPIs) to solve problems with data-driven decisions. I want to focus on the Conversions KPI to increase the number of sessions booked with an artist or the number of tattoos users saved. For example, the conversions for sessions booked will be calculated by the number of bookings divided by the number of visits on the artist’s page. Utilizing this metric will showcase whether a new workflow or feature helped users book a tattoo session with an artist.
